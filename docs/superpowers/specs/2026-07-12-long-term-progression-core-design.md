# Tokensmith Long-Term Progression Core Design

**Date:** 2026-07-12  
**Status:** Approved design  
**Scope:** Long-term model generations, era progression, frontier research, bounded rivals, TUI integration, and save migration

## 1. Context

Tokensmith currently stops meaningful player progression at Gen5 while a strategic-campaign rival can grow without a corresponding ceiling. The late-game failure has two independent causes:

1. `balance.MaxGen` is fixed at 5, and generation cost, training work, and quality values are fixed-size arrays.
2. During an active campaign, normal bounded rival catch-up is disabled. Rival roadmap actions then repeatedly multiply quality (`+15%`, `+8%`, and similar), so a frequently acting company such as OpenAI compounds indefinitely.

This produces states such as OpenAI capability `135185` while most companies remain near `150`, even though the player cannot research beyond Gen5.

The game-time compression makes 20,000 game days approximately 1.4 real days. A long-running save should remain meaningfully playable for at least 3–5 real days and continue indefinitely after that. There must be no forced ending or mandatory prestige point.

## 2. Goals

- Preserve existing Gen1–5 behavior and early-game balance.
- Hand-author Gen6–10 as a paced bridge into the procedural late game.
- Generate Gen11+ specifications without a final generation.
- Group progression into eras with meaningful branch specialization.
- Replace instant generation unlock purchases after Gen5 with long-running frontier research projects.
- Let frontier research and model training share the existing training pool.
- Keep additional compute valuable while applying diminishing returns above each generation's recommended compute.
- Replace permanent rival percentage compounding with a global-frontier band.
- Preserve seven named rivals, their specialties, and rotating era leaders.
- Keep trained model quality values stable while showing relative obsolescence separately.
- Repair existing saves that contain exploded rival quality without discarding their relative rankings.
- Maintain a pure, deterministic `internal/sim` core.

## 3. Non-goals

- Detailed datacenter inventory, server retirement, or chipset replacement.
- Player-designed chips, foundry simulation, yields, production, or chip sales.
- Full financial, compute, staffing, and training simulations for every rival company.
- A new forced victory condition at Gen10, AGI, or ASI.
- Replacing the existing campaign, market segments, model publishing, or prestige systems.

These excluded systems may consume the progression interfaces defined here in later designs.

## 4. Chosen Architecture

The long-term progression core is divided into six bounded responsibilities.

### 4.1 Generation catalog

The balance layer owns generation specifications:

- Gen1–10 are explicit catalog entries.
- Gen11+ are generated by a pure function.
- The catalog never reads or mutates game state.

### 4.2 Era progression

Run-scoped state records the highest unlocked generation, era specialization, supplemental breakthroughs, and the active frontier project.

### 4.3 Frontier project simulation

The simulation core consumes R&D and a player-selected share of training compute over time. It is the only authority for progress, completion, stalls, and generation unlocks.

### 4.4 Global frontier

The global frontier is derived from player progress and an independent time baseline. Rivals move toward bounded targets around this frontier.

### 4.5 View models

Exported simulation view helpers calculate generation labels, era state, frontier difference, effective allocation, ETA, and rival position. TUI pages consume these helpers instead of duplicating formulas.

### 4.6 Versioned persistence

The store recognizes explicit schema versions, backs up old saves, migrates once, validates the result, and writes atomically.

## 5. Generation and Era Model

### 5.1 Era boundaries

| Era | Generations | Role |
|---|---|---|
| I | Gen1–2 | Foundation |
| II | Gen3–4 | Scaling |
| III | Gen5–7 | Industrial frontier |
| IV | Gen8–10 | Neural-scale frontier |
| V+ | Three generations per era | Procedural late game |

For Gen11+, the era is:

```text
era(gen) = 5 + floor((gen - 11) / 3)
```

### 5.2 Generation specification

```go
type GenerationSpec struct {
    Gen                int
    Era                int
    FrontierRnD        float64
    FrontierWork       float64
    TrainRnD           float64
    TrainWork          float64
    QualityScale       float64
    RecommendedCompute float64
    TimeBaselineDay    float64
}
```

`GenerationSpec(gen)` returns an error for `gen < 1`. There is no configured maximum generation.

Existing Gen1–5 values remain unchanged. Gen2–5 retain their existing instant `UnlockTech` path so an early save and its pacing do not change. Frontier projects begin at Gen6.

### 5.3 Gen6–10 calibration

Durations assume sufficient R&D, exactly the recommended compute, and the current `RealSecCompression`. Work values are derived from duration rather than copied as opaque constants:

```text
work = recommendedCompute × targetRealSeconds × RealSecCompression
```

| Gen | Frontier R&D | Frontier duration | Train R&D | Train duration | Quality scale | Recommended compute | Time baseline day |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 6 | 180M | 2h | 120M | 30m | 120 | 500 | 10,000 |
| 7 | 500M | 4h | 350M | 1h | 142 | 1,200 | 14,000 |
| 8 | 1.5B | 6h | 1B | 1.5h | 166 | 3,000 | 20,000 |
| 9 | 4.5B | 8h | 3B | 2h | 192 | 7,500 | 30,000 |
| 10 | 13.5B | 12h | 9B | 3h | 220 | 18,000 | 40,000 |

The Gen1–5 time-baseline days are `0`, `1,000`, `2,500`, `4,500`, and `7,000` respectively.

### 5.4 Gen11+ formulas

For `k = gen - 10`:

```text
FrontierRnD        = Gen10.FrontierRnD        × 2.4^k
FrontierWork       = Gen10.FrontierWork       × 2.6^k
TrainRnD           = Gen10.TrainRnD           × 2.2^k
TrainWork          = Gen10.TrainWork          × 2.8^k
QualityScale       = 220 + 28 × k
RecommendedCompute = 18,000                   × 1.75^k
```

Time-baseline intervals grow rather than absolute days:

```text
interval(Gen10) = baselineDay(Gen10) - baselineDay(Gen9)
interval(gen)   = interval(gen - 1) × 1.35
baselineDay(gen) = baselineDay(gen - 1) + interval(gen)
```

All generated values must be positive, finite, and monotonic. Generation resolution returns a typed range error before any calculation could produce `NaN` or infinity; it never persists invalid values.

Quality grows linearly after Gen10. Costs and work grow geometrically. This preserves visible progress without returning to runaway quality numbers.

## 6. Frontier Research Projects

### 6.1 State

```go
type ProgressionState struct {
    MaxUnlockedGen int
    Frontier       FrontierProject
    Eras           []EraProgress
    Rivals         RivalEraState
}

type FrontierProject struct {
    Active             bool
    TargetGen          int
    RnDTotal           float64
    RnDRemaining       float64
    WorkTotal          float64
    WorkRemaining      float64
    RecommendedCompute float64
    AllocationPct      int
}

type EraProgress struct {
    Era          int
    HasPrimary   bool
    Primary      TechBranch
    UnlockedMask uint8
}

type RivalEraState struct {
    Era     int
    Leaders []string
}
```

Era progress uses an ordered slice rather than a map so serialization and aggregation remain deterministic.

`model.Competitor` retains `Name`, `Quality`, and bounded `Skill`, and adds transient roadmap state:

```go
MomentumPct    [NumQualityDims]float64
MomentumCycles int
```

Momentum is stored as a fraction of the current global frontier, not as an absolute quality value. Existing campaign market modifiers continue to use `Campaign.Active`; rival momentum does not duplicate those effects.

### 6.2 Commands

```go
type StartFrontierProject struct { TargetGen int }
type SetFrontierAllocation struct { Percent int }
type UnlockEraBreakthrough struct { Era int; Branch TechBranch }
```

Rules:

- Only `MaxUnlockedGen + 1` may be started.
- Only one frontier project may be active.
- Allocation is an integer percentage from 0 through 100.
- Starting a project snapshots the resolved generation's required R&D, work, and recommended compute. Total fields remain available for stable progress percentages and ETA.
- Balance changes after a project starts do not rewrite partial progress.
- A project cannot be cancelled; allocation may be set to zero to pause it.

A new run initializes `MaxUnlockedGen = 1`. Successfully unlocking one of the existing Gen2–5 technology nodes updates `MaxUnlockedGen` in the same pure command application, keeping the legacy tech list and the new progression state consistent.

### 6.3 Shared training pool

```text
frontierAllocated = EffectiveTraining × AllocationPct / 100
modelAllocated    = EffectiveTraining × (100 - AllocationPct) / 100
```

If no frontier project or no model training exists, its assigned share remains idle. The simulation does not silently reallocate compute because that would make player intent and ETA unpredictable.

Existing model training remains linear. Diminishing returns apply only to frontier research.

### 6.4 Diminishing returns

For allocated compute `A` and recommended compute `R`:

```text
if A <= R:
    effective = A
else:
    effective = R + sqrt((A - R) × R)
```

There is no hard duration timer or hard compute cap. Additional compute always helps, but its marginal value declines beyond the era's recommendation.

### 6.5 Streaming R&D consumption

R&D is spent in proportion to completed work:

```text
rndPerWork = RnDRemaining / WorkRemaining
workByCompute = diminishedCompute × dt
workByRnD = Resources.RnD / rndPerWork
workDone = min(workByCompute, workByRnD, WorkRemaining)

Resources.RnD  -= workDone × rndPerWork
RnDRemaining   -= workDone × rndPerWork
WorkRemaining  -= workDone
```

Small floating-point residuals within the shared simulation epsilon complete together. If R&D is insufficient, project work does not advance. Reserved frontier compute remains reserved and the view reports the stall.

On completion, `MaxUnlockedGen` becomes `TargetGen` and the frontier project resets to its zero state.

## 7. Era Breakthroughs

Procedural era breakthroughs begin in Era III. Era I–II continue to use the existing fixed technology catalog, preserving Gen1–5 behavior. Every era from Era III onward exposes one breakthrough in each existing branch:

- Algorithms
- Infrastructure
- Business
- Alignment

The first branch researched in an era becomes its primary specialization and costs the base amount. Every other branch remains available at `1.75 × base cost`. Primary status changes price and timing, not effect magnitude.

The base cost is deterministic:

```text
EraBreakthroughBaseCost = 0.25 × FrontierRnD(first frontier-project generation in the era)
```

For Era III the first frontier-project generation is Gen6; for Era IV it is Gen8; for Era V+ it is the era's first generation.

Infinite repeated nodes do not multiply one another directly. Their effects are aggregated from the number of unlocked era breakthroughs per branch:

```text
Algorithms quality output = 1 + 0.05 × sqrt(algorithmBreakthroughs)
Infrastructure compute     = 1 + 0.08 × sqrt(infraBreakthroughs)
Business user growth       = 1 + 0.06 × sqrt(businessBreakthroughs)
Business reference price   = 1 + 0.03 × sqrt(businessBreakthroughs)
Alignment safety output    = 1 + 0.06 × sqrt(alignmentBreakthroughs)
Alignment incident effect  = 1 / (1 + 0.10 × sqrt(alignmentBreakthroughs))
```

These aggregate era effects combine once with existing fixed tech, star, event, and prestige effects. Algorithms and alignment quality effects apply only when a new model completes training; they never rewrite an existing model.

The next era opens only after:

1. the final generation of the current era is unlocked; and
2. at least two of the four era breakthroughs are unlocked.

The remaining breakthroughs may be purchased later. No early decision permanently traps a long-running save.

Fixed Gen1–5 technology IDs and effects remain in `UnlockedTech`. Procedural era progress is stored structurally in `ProgressionState.Eras`; the game does not append an unbounded list of generated string IDs.

## 8. Global Frontier

### 8.1 Player and time sources

For each quality dimension:

```text
GlobalFrontier[d] = max(PlayerFrontier[d], TimeFrontier[d])
```

`PlayerFrontier[d]` is the best value among online player models and does not mutate those models.

The time frontier interpolates between generation baseline days. Its base dimension value is scaled from the existing competitor baseline:

```text
TimeFrontier[d] = CompetitorBaseQuality
                × InterpolatedGenerationQualityScale
                / Gen1.QualityScale
```

This preserves the current starting relationship: rivals begin near quality 8 while a Gen1 model's quality scale is 25. It does not assume a player allocation across the four dimensions.

If the player advances faster than time, the industry begins catching up to the player. Rivals still move gradually toward targets and never snap to the new frontier in one tick.

### 8.2 Offline advancement

Player economy settlement continues to use the existing offline path. Global-frontier and rival catch-up receive a separate cap:

- at most eight real hours converted through `RealSecCompression`; and
- at most the residual industry time until the next generation `TimeBaselineDay` (never cross the next baseline in one settle — not the full current-generation interval width).

The smaller allowance wins. Excess offline time is not replayed later. Across successive sessions the residual ratchets forward, so progress continues without banking dropped backlog.

## 9. Rival League

### 9.1 Shared engine

Normal play and strategic campaigns use the same bounded rival engine. A campaign no longer disables ordinary frontier tracking.

Each company's existing `Skill` becomes a bounded specialty vector. Values are calibrated within `0.92–1.08`.

For every rival and dimension, leader and momentum values are frontier-relative fractions:

```text
targetPct = clamp(
    Specialty[d] + LeaderBonusPct + MomentumPct[d],
    0.85,
    1.15,
)
target = GlobalFrontier[d] × targetPct
```

The rival quality approaches this target with the configured catch-up rate. Every public rival update re-applies the band invariant.

### 9.2 Era leaders

At each era boundary, the simulation deterministically selects two or three distinct leaders using:

- the saved simulation RNG state;
- the era number; and
- company specialties.

The selected names are persisted in `RivalEraState`, so unrelated future RNG use cannot change leaders after reload. A leader receives at most `LeaderBonusPct = 0.04` before the final band clamp.

### 9.3 Roadmap actions

Roadmap actions no longer multiply stored quality. A due action:

1. closes a configured fraction of the remaining distance to the rival's bounded target;
2. clamps the result to the `85%–115%` band;
3. adds an existing campaign market modifier for two or three cycles; and
4. records a bounded `MomentumPct` that decays linearly over its configured cycles and resets to zero across era transitions.

Repeated actions can rotate rankings and create temporary pressure, but cannot produce exponential quality growth.

## 10. Stable Model Quality and Relative Views

Training completion continues to write fixed quality values to the model. Later frontier movement never rewrites them.

View helpers expose, per dimension:

```text
absoluteQuality = stored model quality
frontierDeltaPct = absoluteQuality / GlobalFrontier - 1
generationGap = equivalent frontier generation - model generation
```

The equivalent generation uses the generation catalog's quality scale and interpolation. It is explanatory only and does not change market calculations.

Example:

```text
能力 184
當前前沿 201
落後 8.5% · 約 1 世代
```

## 11. TUI Design

### 11.1 Technology page

The page uses the approved era-ladder layout:

- past eras are collapsed summaries;
- the current era is expanded;
- only the next era is previewed;
- procedural eras do not increase page height without bound.

Keys:

- `↑` / `↓`: select a visible node;
- `[` / `]`: move between eras;
- `Enter`: unlock a fixed node, start the selected frontier project, or unlock an era breakthrough;
- `+` / `-`: adjust frontier allocation by ten percentage points.

The global left/right page navigation remains unchanged.

### 11.2 Overview and compute pages

Both pages show the same authoritative view data:

- frontier project generation, progress, and ETA;
- model training generation, progress, and ETA;
- effective training compute;
- frontier/model allocation split;
- recommended compute and post-diminishing effective compute;
- R&D stall state.

The compute page does not own the allocation rule; it only renders it.

### 11.3 Model page

Model details retain fixed four-dimensional values and add current frontier, percentage difference, and approximate generation difference.

### 11.4 Market page

The rival list adds:

- global frontier and current era;
- leader badges;
- percentage above or below the frontier;
- active roadmap market-effect duration;
- a visible explanation that rivals are bounded to `85%–115%`.

### 11.5 Feedback

Persistent conditions such as insufficient R&D appear in cards and pressure lines rather than repeated toast notifications. ETA is omitted with a concrete reason when allocation, compute, or R&D makes it undefined.

## 12. Errors and Invariants

New typed errors include:

- invalid generation specification;
- invalid frontier target;
- frontier project already active;
- no active frontier project;
- allocation outside `0–100`;
- era not open;
- era breakthrough already unlocked;
- insufficient R&D;
- unmet era prerequisites.

Core invariants:

- Generation values are positive, finite, and monotonic.
- Allocation is always within `0–100`; frontier plus model allocation equals 100%.
- Project progress and resources never become negative.
- A project unlocks exactly one next generation.
- Rival values stay within `85%–115%` of the global frontier per dimension.
- Model quality never changes after training completion.
- The same state, config, `dt`, inputs, and RNG state produce the same output.

## 13. Prestige

Prestige remains optional and player-triggered. Reaching Gen10, AGI, ASI, or a procedural era never forces settlement.

A prestige reset clears:

- highest unlocked generation;
- era progression and specialization;
- active frontier project;
- run-scoped technologies and existing run-scoped state already cleared today.

Patents and permanent prestige nodes remain. Higher reached eras may increase the patent award calculation, but continuing the existing run is always valid.

## 14. Save Schema and Migration

### 14.1 Versioned envelope

New saves use an explicit envelope:

```go
type SaveFile struct {
    SchemaVersion int             `json:"schemaVersion"`
    State         model.GameState `json:"state"`
}
```

The loader recognizes the current unwrapped `GameState` JSON as schema version 0.

### 14.2 Migration steps

Before mutation, the loader writes one backup beside the save, such as `save.json.v0.bak`. Migration then:

1. derives `MaxUnlockedGen` from generation tech IDs, completed models, and active training;
2. creates era records for already reached generations;
3. derives the current global frontier from game time and player models;
4. ranks rivals independently in each quality dimension;
5. maps those ranks to `88%`, `92%`, `96%`, `100%`, `104%`, `109%`, and `114%` of the frontier;
6. clamps legacy `Skill` values into the new `0.92–1.08` specialty range while preserving each company's strongest dimension;
7. clears legacy permanent roadmap compounding state;
8. initializes persisted era leaders with zero momentum;
9. validates finite, non-negative resources and progress; and
10. atomically writes the current schema.

Ranking is preserved even when absolute legacy values are invalid. Applying the migration again to the same migrated state produces no changes.

If backup, migration, validation, or write fails, the original save remains untouched and the error is shown. The game never silently starts a new run after a migration failure.

## 15. Testing Strategy

### 15.1 Generation and era tests

- Existing Gen1–5 specifications and behavior remain unchanged.
- Gen6–10 match the approved calibration table.
- Gen11–100 values are finite, positive, and monotonic.
- Era boundaries and three-generation procedural eras are correct.
- A new era requires the current era's final generation and two breakthroughs.
- Primary and supplemental breakthrough costs use `1.0×` and `1.75×`.

### 15.2 Frontier project tests

- Allocation conservation across `0`, intermediate, and `100` percent.
- Linear compute at and below recommendation.
- Square-root diminishing returns above recommendation.
- R&D shortage prevents work advancement.
- Allocation changes preserve accumulated progress.
- Project completion unlocks exactly the next generation.
- Invalid commands return the exact typed errors without mutating input state.

### 15.3 Rival tests

- Reproduce the legacy OpenAI repeated-action scenario.
- Run at least 100,000 campaign cycles and assert every rival dimension remains within the band.
- Repeated roadmap actions never produce exponential stored quality.
- Era leaders rotate deterministically and survive save/load.
- Company specialties still influence dimension rankings.
- Campaign and non-campaign paths share the same band invariant.

### 15.4 Long-run calibration tests

A deterministic reference-economy fixture verifies ranges rather than one brittle exact generation. The fixture starts at day 7,000 with Gen5 unlocked, sufficient R&D, no optional tech/star/prestige multipliers, and exactly the current target generation's recommended compute. It allocates 100% to frontier research until unlock, then 100% to training one model of that generation before starting the next frontier project. It does not apply offline catch-up.

Under that scripted policy:

- at day 20,000, highest unlocked generation is Gen8–10;
- at day 43,200–72,000, highest unlocked generation is Gen10–13;
- extra compute improves completion time but exhibits diminishing marginal gains;
- no generation or era forces a run end.

### 15.5 View, TUI, and persistence tests

- Advancing the global frontier does not mutate stored model quality.
- Relative frontier and equivalent-generation views update correctly.
- Era cursor order, collapsed history, narrow viewport, scrolling, and new keys are covered.
- Old saves preserve rival rankings and normalize values.
- Migration is idempotent and preserves corrupt-save evidence on failure.
- Offline frontier advancement respects both caps.

Final verification requires:

```bash
go test ./...
go vet ./...
go build ./...
```

## 16. Acceptance Criteria

The design is implemented successfully when:

1. A player can progress from Gen5 through Gen10 and into generated Gen11+ eras.
2. Day 20,000 no longer represents a hard player cap.
3. No rival quality can escape the global-frontier band, including after long campaign simulations.
4. Frontier research visibly consumes R&D and the selected training-compute share.
5. Model training can continue concurrently with the remaining compute share.
6. Era specialization creates an early discount without permanently locking other branches.
7. Model quality remains stable while obsolescence is shown separately.
8. Existing exploded saves are backed up, normalized, and remain playable.
9. Prestige remains optional and the run has no forced terminal generation.
10. All unit, long-run, TUI, migration, vet, and build checks pass.
